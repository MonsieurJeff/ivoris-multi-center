# The Transition

**The Pivot from Pipeline to Multi-Center**

This is the most important moment in the presentation. It's where you show initiative and set up the impressive second half.

---

## The Moment

You've just finished demonstrating ivoris-pipeline. The main challenge is complete. Now you need to smoothly transition to the extension.

---

## The Full Pivot Script

Practice this until it feels natural:

> "All acceptance criteria met. Clean architecture - database adapter, extraction service, data model. **The main challenge is complete.**"

*[PAUSE - let this land]*

> "So the main challenge is done. But then I started thinking..."

*[Change energy - more curious, engaged]*

> "Clinero doesn't manage one dental practice. They manage many. What happens when you need to extract from 30 centers? 50? 100?"

*[Build tension]*

> "And here's the thing about Ivoris that makes this **really** interesting..."

*[Dramatic pause]*

> "Each Ivoris installation can have **randomly generated** table and column names."

*[Let that sink in]*

> "KARTEI_MN in Munich. KARTEI_8Y in Berlin. KARTEI_XQ4 in Hamburg. No two centers have the same schema. You can't write one SQL query that works everywhere."

*[Transition to solution]*

> "I decided to solve this properly. Let me show you."

*[Navigate to ivoris-multi-center]*

---

## Why This Works

1. **Completes the first story** - "The main challenge is complete" closes Act 2
2. **Shows initiative** - "But then I started thinking..." shows you went beyond
3. **Creates tension** - "Randomly generated names" is a genuine challenge
4. **Grounds in reality** - "Clinero manages many practices" connects to real business
5. **Transitions naturally** - "Let me show you" leads into the demo

---

## Common Mistakes

### Don't Say:
- "I know you didn't ask for this, but..."
- "I had some extra time so I..."
- "This might be overkill..."
- "I'm not sure if this is relevant..."

### Do Say:
- "But then I started thinking..."
- "In production, you'd need to handle..."
- "I decided to solve this properly..."
- "Let me show you what happens at scale..."

## Energy Shift

| Before Pivot | During Pivot | After Pivot |
|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| Calm, professional | Curious, building | Confident, energized |
| "The main challenge is complete" | "But what about 30 centers?" | "Let me show you" |

---

## Physical Cues

- **Before:** Settled, showing completed work
- **During:** Lean in slightly, eyebrows up (curiosity)
- **After:** More energy, faster pace as you show multi-center

---

## The Short Version

If you need a compressed pivot (10 seconds):

> "Main challenge done. But what about 30 centers with random schema names? I solved that too. Let me show you."

---

## Practice Drill

Say these three times each, out loud:

1. "The main challenge is complete."
2. "But then I started thinking... what happens at scale?"
3. "Each installation can have randomly generated table names."
4. "I decided to solve this properly. Let me show you."

The pivot should feel as natural as telling a story to a friend.

## If You Forget

Then continue with the multi-center demo. The audience will follow.
